Who We Are

About SAYA

A dynamic, youth-driven movement dedicated to transforming agriculture in Zambia and beyond through innovative programs, strategic partnerships, and community empowerment.

Our Vision

To create a generation of empowered young agricultural leaders who drive sustainable development, enhance food security, and pioneer technological innovations across Zambia and the broader African continent.

Our Mission

To empower youth and farming communities through comprehensive knowledge sharing, sustainable agriculture practices, seamless technology adoption, improved market access, entrepreneurial training, and inclusive collaboration spanning all agricultural value chains.

SAYA is committed to working in alignment with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), particularly:

  • SDG 2 (Zero Hunger): Through sustainable agriculture practices, improved crop yields, and enhanced food security initiatives across Zambian communities.
  • SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-being): By promoting healthy farming practices, access to nutritious food, and safe agricultural technologies that support community health.
  • SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth): Through youth employment opportunities, entrepreneurial training, and economic empowerment in the agricultural sector.
  • SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production): Via sustainable production methods, reduced chemical pesticide use, and responsible resource management in farming operations.
  • SDG 13 (Climate Action): Through climate-resilient farming techniques, environmental conservation, and adaptation strategies to combat climate change impacts.

Additionally, we align with the Zambian Government's vision to position agriculture as a cornerstone of economic development, contributing significantly to GDP growth, job creation, and national food security.

Five-Year Strategic Plan (2025-2030)

SAYA's comprehensive five-year roadmap outlines our phased expansion and impact strategy across Zambia, starting with our inaugural chapter in Chililabombwe.

Year 1 (2026): Foundation & First Chapter Launch

  • Establish Chililabombwe Chapter as our flagship regional hub
  • Cater to youth in Chililabombwe, Kitwe, Chingola, and Kasumbalesa districts
  • Launch initial training programs and technology adoption initiatives
  • Build partnerships with local stakeholders and government entities
  • Achieve 500 youth participants and 10 cooperative establishments

Year 2 (2027): Expansion & Technology Integration

  • Launch additional chapters in Copperbelt and Northwestern provinces
  • Implement advanced digital platforms and IoT solutions
  • Scale training programs to 2,000 participants
  • Establish 20 youth-led cooperatives with sustainable practices

Year 3 (2028): National Scaling

  • Expand to Lusaka and Southern provinces
  • Develop comprehensive market linkages and value chain integration
  • Reach 4,000 trained youth and 30 operational cooperatives
  • Launch innovation hubs for agricultural technology development

Year 4 (2029): Impact Consolidation

  • Cover Eastern, Northern, and Luapula provinces
  • Strengthen international partnerships and funding streams
  • Achieve 7,000 participants and 40 cooperatives
  • Implement large-scale climate-resilient farming programs

Year 5 (2030): Sustainability & Legacy

  • Complete national coverage across all 10 provinces
  • Establish SAYA as a leading youth agricultural movement in Africa
  • Reach 10,000+ empowered youth and 50+ thriving cooperatives
  • Develop self-sustaining business models and knowledge transfer systems
  • Celebrate measurable impacts in food security and economic development

Digital Innovation

Major Visitors Advance Aquaculture Youth Empowerment

Date: December 17, 2025

Yesterday, we had the rare privilege of receiving major visitors in the area of agriculture feed supply and aquaculture youth empowerment. Our first visitor was the LATICE PROJECT COORDINATOR, Mr. Francis, who came to fulfill the WYEEFIMA PROJECT commitment by providing 3,000 Tilapia fingerlings and 28 bags of fish feed to 20 youths through HAPITEC INVESTMENTS LTD and its directors.

This initiative is entirely facilitated by LATTICE under a project called WYEEFIMA, which aims to empower women, youths, people with disabilities, and other fish traders. It also aims to expand market access, linkages, and financial access in fish farming and trade. The empowerment through HAPITEC INVESTMENTS LTD is led by our Director of Administration, Mrs. Prudence Molobeka.

Our second visitor was the Farm Feed technical experts. It was a very progressive discussion and coaching, which the team will not take lightly, but as a major contribution to the sustainability and going concern of our business. We also anticipate a lot of positive changes, as both our primary focus is on achieving a good yield that brings revenue to both our businesses and ensures customer satisfaction.

SAYA Launches Flagship Chapter in Chililabombwe

Date: January 15, 2025

SAYA has officially launched its flagship chapter in Chililabombwe, marking the beginning of our five-year expansion plan across Zambia. The launch event brought together over 200 youth leaders, farmers, and stakeholders to celebrate this milestone in sustainable agriculture development.

The chapter will serve as a hub for training programs, technology adoption, and community outreach initiatives in the Copperbelt region. This strategic location was chosen due to its agricultural significance and the concentration of youth involved in farming activities.

Key Achievements from the Launch Event:

  • Registration of 150+ youth members committed to sustainable agriculture
  • Partnership agreements with 8 local cooperatives and farming organizations
  • Allocation of demonstration farms for hands-on training and technology testing
  • Establishment of mentorship programs connecting experienced farmers with youth learners
  • Launch of digital farmer platform access for all registered members

What's Next: The Chililabombwe chapter will host its first training cohort beginning February 2025, with focus on sustainable maize production, vegetable farming, and value-added processing. The chapter aims to reach 500 youth participants within the first year and expand to neighboring communities.

Partnership with Hapitec Strengthens Technology Integration

Date: February 3, 2025

SAYA and Hapitec Investment Limited have formalized their strategic partnership, combining SAYA's youth-led initiatives with Hapitec's cutting-edge agricultural technology solutions. This collaboration will accelerate the adoption of digital tools across Zambian farming communities.

The partnership includes joint development of mobile applications, IoT sensor networks, and data analytics platforms to support precision agriculture and sustainable farming practices.

Partnership Components:

  • Digital Farmer Platform: Mobile app providing real-time market prices, weather forecasts, and agronomic advice
  • IoT Sensor Integration: Smart soil moisture and temperature sensors for precision irrigation
  • Data Analytics: Crop yield prediction and optimization tools using AI
  • Training & Support: Technology training for 500+ farmers across 20 communities
  • Equipment Access: Subsidized technology packages for early adopters

Expected Impact: This partnership is projected to increase farm productivity by 35-50%, reduce input costs by 20-30%, and enable farmers to access premium markets through digital connections. The first pilot phase will engage 200 farmers across Chililabombwe and neighboring regions.

Timeline: Full platform deployment is scheduled for Q3 2025, with initial pilot testing beginning in March 2025.

Youth Agricultural Leadership Program Kicks Off

Date: February 20, 2025

The first cohort of SAYA's Youth Agricultural Leadership Program (YALP) has begun training sessions in Chililabombwe. Fifty young leaders are participating in intensive workshops covering sustainable farming techniques, business development, and leadership skills.

The program, supported by the Ministry of Agriculture, aims to equip participants with the knowledge and tools needed to establish successful agricultural cooperatives and contribute to Zambia's food security goals.

Program Structure:

  • Duration: 6-month intensive program (February - July 2025)
  • Module 1: Sustainable Farming Practices & Climate-Smart Agriculture
  • Module 2: Value Addition & Agri-Business Development
  • Module 3: Cooperative Management & Leadership
  • Module 4: Digital Literacy & Technology Adoption
  • Module 5: Marketing, Supply Chains & Market Access

Participant Profile: The 50 selected participants are between ages 18-40, with prior farming experience and demonstrated commitment to community development. Participants include 40% women to ensure gender balance in agricultural leadership.

Support Mechanisms: Participants receive monthly stipends, access to demonstration farms, mentorship from experienced farmers, and business startup grants upon program completion. The top 5 graduates will receive ZK 50,000 each to establish their own agri-enterprises.

Expansion Plans: Based on the success of this cohort, SAYA plans to launch 5 additional YALP cohorts across different provinces by end of 2025, reaching 500+ young agricultural leaders.
