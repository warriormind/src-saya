About Hapitec Investments Ltd

Hapitec Investments Ltd is a privately owned company registered under the Zambian Companies Act No. 10 Of 2017. It deals in lucrative piggery and is engaged in other agricultural and animal production businesses, including crop farming, livestock farming, trading of agricultural products, and other related businesses. The company is committed to sustainable agricultural practices and employs the latest technologies to ensure high quality produce and livestock products. It also has a highly qualified and experienced team of professionals and provides high quality agricultural products to its customers.

Programs & Services

Training & Certification

Modular courses (4–12 weeks) covering precision farming, animal husbandry, digital tools, and agribusiness management with hands-on labs and certificates for Hapitec-ready technicians.

On-Farm Technology Deployment

Installation of IoT sensors, automated feeders, and mobile apps with local youth technicians providing installation and maintenance services.

Market Linkage & Aggregation

Aggregation hubs and digital marketplaces connecting smallholder produce to Hapitec processing and national buyers.

Technology & Innovations

  • HapTrack Mobile: Field data capture, yield forecasting and farmer management (pilot-ready)
  • IoT Piggery Suite: Environmental sensors, automated feeding logs, and health alerts reduce mortality and improve weight gain
  • MarketIQ Dashboard: Real-time pricing, demand signals and supply chain traceability for producers and buyers

Monitoring, Evaluation & KPIs

  • Adoption Rate: % of trained youth actively using Hapitec solutions within 6 months
  • Productivity Increase: Average % yield/livestock weight gain per cohort
  • Income Uplift: Average change in household income among program participants
  • Customer Retention: Repeat purchase and service subscription rates
  • Environmental Impact: Water and feed efficiency improvements and reduced waste

All KPIs are tracked via the MarketIQ Dashboard and presented in quarterly impact reports.

🐷 Pig Processing Value Chain (Active)

Current Implementation: SAYA and Hapitec have established an integrated livestock processing value chain for sustainable pork production.

Processing Stages:

  • Stage 1 - Livestock Production: Youth farmers raise healthy pigs using sustainable feeding practices and veterinary care
  • Stage 2 - Slaughtering & Butchering: Professional processing facility with hygiene standards for quality meat cuts
  • Stage 3 - Pork Products: Fresh pork cuts distributed to local markets and retailers with cold chain management
  • Stage 4 - Value-Added Sausages: Production of specialty sausages using traditional recipes and modern processing techniques with premium packaging
  • Stage 5 - Market Distribution: Direct sales to restaurants, supermarkets, and consumers; online ordering available

Income Growth Model:

Farmer Revenue Increase: From farm-gate pig sales to premium sausage production:

  • Raw Pig Sales: ZK 150-200 per kg (farm-gate price)
  • Fresh Pork Retail: ZK 250-350 per kg (+50-75% margin)
  • Premium Sausages: ZK 400-600 per kg (+100-200% margin)
  • Total Value Addition: 3-4x income increase through processing chain

Current Success Metrics:

  • 150+ youth farmers engaged in pig production
  • 2 modern processing facilities operational
  • 500+ kg sausages produced monthly
  • 40+ regular retail/restaurant clients
  • Average farmer income: ZK 8,000-12,000/month (from ZK 2,000-3,000)

🌽 Maize Processing Initiative (Planned)

Future Implementation (2027-2028): SAYA plans to launch a comprehensive maize processing value chain following the successful pig processing model.

Planned Processing Stages:

  • Stage 1 - Maize Cultivation: Sustainable farming with improved varieties and soil management
  • Stage 2 - Milling & Processing: Modern mills for flour production with quality grading
  • Stage 3 - Value-Added Products: Fortified flour, cornmeal, polenta, animal feed concentrate
  • Stage 4 - Premium Packaging: Branded, packaged products for retail distribution
  • Stage 5 - Market Diversification: Direct consumer sales, institutional contracts (schools, hospitals), export potential

Expected Value Addition:

  • Subsistence Sale: ZK 150-200 per bag (50kg) (farm-gate)
  • Processed Flour: ZK 300-400 per bag (+50-100% margin)
  • Premium Products: ZK 500-700 per bag (+200-250% margin)
  • Target: 4-5x income increase similar to pig processing

Implementation Timeline:

  • 2027: Feasibility study, market analysis, equipment procurement
  • 2027-2028: Pilot with 500 farmers, establish 3-5 processing centers
  • 2028+: Scale to 2,000+ farmers across 20+ communities

Strategic Rationale:

Maize is Zambia's primary staple crop with reliable production and strong market demand. Processing value addition addresses:

  • Post-harvest losses (currently 15-20%)
  • Farmer income vulnerability during low-price seasons
  • Rural employment creation (500+ jobs estimated)
  • Nutrition enhancement through fortified flour products

Your Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about SAYA, membership, programs, and more

SAYA stands for Sustainable Agriculture Youth Alliance. We are a youth-driven movement focused on empowering young leaders to transform agriculture through sustainable practices, technology adoption, and community engagement across Zambia and beyond.

Anyone aged 18-35 years with an interest in agriculture and sustainable development can join SAYA. We welcome youth from all backgrounds including farmers, students, professionals, and anyone committed to youth empowerment and community service. Basic understanding of agricultural practices is helpful but not required - we provide training.

SAYA members enjoy access to training programs and workshops, networking opportunities with industry leaders, participation in sustainable farming projects, technology and innovation resources, leadership development and mentorship, and certificate of membership with recognition. Members also get priority access to new initiatives and community outreach activities.

SAYA operates under transparent and accountable leadership. Our governance structure includes a Board of Directors composed of experienced leaders in agriculture and sustainable development, an Executive Committee that oversees operations, specialized Technical Committees focusing on technology and training, and Regional Chapters in various provinces to ensure local representation and community engagement.

SAYA offers comprehensive training in sustainable farming techniques, business development and entrepreneurship, leadership skills, technology adoption and digital tools, market linkages and value addition, cooperative formation and management, and environmental sustainability practices. Our Youth Agricultural Leadership Program (YALP) is our flagship training initiative combining theory with hands-on experience.

SAYA is funded through strategic partnerships with organizations like the Ministry of Agriculture, development agencies, private sector partners including Hapitec Investment Limited, grants from agricultural development programs, and member contributions. All funding is managed transparently and dedicated to advancing our mission of youth empowerment and sustainable agriculture.

SAYA has formalized a strategic partnership with Hapitec Investment Limited to integrate cutting-edge agricultural technology into youth-led initiatives. This collaboration includes joint development of mobile applications, IoT sensor networks, and data analytics platforms. Hapitec provides technology training, digital tools, and market linkage support to SAYA members, accelerating adoption of precision agriculture and sustainable farming practices.

You can apply for SAYA membership through our website membership application form. Simply fill out the form with your personal details, select your membership category (Youth, Student, Professional, or Farmer), and explain your interest in sustainable agriculture. Once submitted, our team will review your application and contact you within 2-3 business days. Visit our Membership section to get started.

SAYA has regional chapters across multiple provinces in Zambia, with our flagship chapter in Chililabombwe launched in January 2025. We are actively expanding our presence across the country as part of our five-year expansion plan. Our chapters serve as hubs for training programs, technology adoption, and community outreach. Contact us to find the chapter nearest to you or to explore opportunities in your area.

WYEEFIMA is a LATTICE-facilitated project aimed at empowering women, youths, people with disabilities, and fish traders in aquaculture. The project provides fingerlings, fish feed, training, and market access support to expand fish farming opportunities. Through partnerships with organizations like HAPITEC INVESTMENTS LTD, WYEEFIMA helps youth establish sustainable aquaculture enterprises and linkages to broader agricultural networks.
